Course Description: The Advocacy Cycle

The AI Policy Entrepreneur doesn't just study policy; they launch it. This course is designed as a six-stage "deployment" cycle where students identify a local point of failure and build an AI-powered advocacy engine to fix it.

We move from the broad **Advocacy Ecosystem** (mapping power) to the **Strategic Workstreams** (gathering intelligence and designing solutions), down to the granular **Advocacy Protocol** (the prompts and narratives that influence decision-makers).

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Architecting Influence: Deploy AI systems for persistent jurisdictional monitoring.
  • Adapting Policy Frameworks: Use LLMs to translate technical policy needs into politically viable drafts.
  • Simulating Resistance: Use agentic AI to predict and counter opposition strategies before deployment.
  • Ethical Advocacy: Critically assess the boundaries of automated influence and democratic participation.

Course Assignments

Total Points: 100

  • Issue Monitoring Dashboard (20 Points): Build an automated system to track a specific policy issue across jurisdictions using automated monitoring of minutes and filings.
  • Legislative Drafting Portfolio (25 Points): Retrieve a model policy and use AI to draft variants, searching for the "Goldilocks zone" of support through comparative analysis.
  • Regulatory Comment Bot (25 Points): Design a system to generate 1,000+ unique, substantive regulatory comments, addressing the ethics of automated participation.
  • Final Project: Policy Simulation: The Deployment (30 Points): Move the "Code" of your proposal through a simulated (or real) legislative gauntlet.
