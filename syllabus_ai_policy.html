Policy Entrepreneurship with AI

Special Topic | Spring 2026

Instructor information

Dr. José Marichal (he/him/his)
Professor of Political Science

Course Description: The Enactment Engine

The AI Policy Entrepreneur doesn't just study policy; they launch it. This course is designed as a six-stage "deployment" cycle where students identify a local point of failure and build an AI-powered tactical engine to fix it.

Following the C4 Architecture Model, we move from the broad Political Context (mapping power) to the functional Containers of advocacy (building the workstreams), down to the granular Components of influence (the prompts and scripts that change minds).

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Architecting Influence: Deploy AI systems for persistent jurisdictional monitoring.
  • Refactoring Legislation: Use LLMs to translate technical policy needs into politically viable drafts.
  • Simulating Resistance: Use agentic AI to "Red Team" advocacy strategies before deployment.
  • Ethical Debugging: Critically assess the boundaries of "Automated Advocacy."

Course Assignments

Total Points: 100

  • Issue Monitoring Dashboard (20 Points): Build an automated system to track a specific policy issue across jurisdictions using automated monitoring of minutes and filings.
  • Legislative Drafting Portfolio (25 Points): Retrieve a model policy and use AI to draft variants, searching for the "Goldilocks zone" of support through comparative analysis.
  • Regulatory Comment Bot (25 Points): Design a system to generate 1,000+ unique, substantive regulatory comments, addressing the ethics of automated participation.
  • Final Project: Policy Simulation: The Deployment (30 Points): Move the "Code" of your proposal through a simulated (or real) legislative gauntlet.
