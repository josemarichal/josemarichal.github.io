Participatory Design Voice Simulator

Interactive Voice Conversations with Simulated Stakeholders

Project Overview

The **Participatory Design Voice Simulator** is an interactive AI tool that allows policy makers, students, and citizens to "test drive" their ideas against simulated stakeholders. Unlike text-based tools, this simulator uses voice-to-voice interaction to create a visceral, realistic town-hall experience.

Users speak their proposals into the microphone, and AI agents—configured with distinct personas like a "Skeptical Union Rep" or a "Visionary Urban Planner"—respond audibly, raising concerns, support, or counter-arguments in real-time.

Launch the Voice Simulator

Why Voice?

Text-based feedback is often abstract and easy to dismiss. Hearing a voice—with intonation, hesitation, and emotion—triggers a different level of empathy and engagement. This tool leverages:

  • Speech-to-Text (Whisper): To accurately capture the nuance of your spoken proposal.
  • Persona-Based LLMs (Qwen/Llama): To generate responses that reflect deep, specific worldviews rather than generic "AI" answers.
  • Text-to-Speech (MMS/gTTS): To give those personas a voice, making the feedback feel immediate and human.

How It Works

  1. Choose a Stakeholder: Select who you want to pitch to (e.g., A City Manager concerned with budget, or a Community Organizer concerned with displacement).
  2. Speak Your Idea: Use your microphone to explain your policy or project.
  3. Listen to the Response: The AI will process your input and "speak back" to you, offering a critique or perspective from that specific stakeholder's point of view.
