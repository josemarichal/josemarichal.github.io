Reflective Artifact Portfolio

Connecting Global Rights to Digital Culture

Assignment Overview

Global politics is not just something that happens in treaties or distant capitals; it reverberates through the culture we consume every day. The Reflective Artifact Portfolio asks you to find a digital artifact—a TikTok video, a song, a meme, a scene from a show, or a news clip—that embodies the specific "right" or political concept we are discussing that week.

You will curate these artifacts and write a reflection for each one, explaining how this piece of culture engages with complex ideas of power, freedom, inequality, or security. This assignment bridges the gap between abstract political theory and the digital world you inhabit.

Weekly "AI-Proof" Requirements

To ensure this work represents your unique perspective and engagement, every reflection must adhere to the following three "Human Integrity" constraints. Reflections that fail to meet these will not be graded.

Constraint 1: The "Algorithm's Path" (Context)

AI can analyze a text, but it cannot know your life. You must explicitly explain how you found this artifact. Did it appear on your "For You" page? Why does the algorithm think you wanted to see it? Is it a song you've loved for years? Connect the artifact to your specific browsing habits, history, or personal taste.

Constraint 2: The "Timestamp" Rule (Specificity)

AI summaries are often generic. You must reference a specific timestamp (e.g., 0:42) or a specific visual/audio detail that is not in the title or description. Describe the tone of voice, the background scenery, a specific camera cut, or a subtle lyric. Prove you watched/listened with human eyes and ears.

Constraint 3: The "Classroom Echo" (Synthesis)

You must connect the artifact not just to the "topic" generally, but to a specific moment from our class discussion that week. Quote a question a peer asked, a specific example I gave in the lecture, or a debate we had in the room. This grounds the reflection in our shared physical reality.

Structure of Your Reflection (300-400 words)

  1. The Artifact: Provide a link and a brief description.
  2. The Discovery (Context): How did you find it? Why did it cross your path? (Constraint 1)
  3. The Analysis (Specificity): Analyze a specific moment (timestamp/lyric). How does this specific detail illustrate the week's theme? (Constraint 2)
  4. The Connection (Synthesis): How does this artifact answer or complicate a question raised in class this week? (Constraint 3)

Example Themes & Artifacts

  • Right to Privacy: A TikTok joking about "My FBI Agent" watching me -> Analyze how surveillance is normalized through humor.
  • Right to Protest: A music video featuring imagery of civil unrest -> Analyze the aestheticization of dissent.
  • Right to Truth: A deepfake video or a "community note" on Twitter -> Analyze the fragile nature of shared reality.
