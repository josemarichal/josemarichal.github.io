AI Stakeholder Analysis Tool

Simulating Multi-Stakeholder Perspectives for Policy Assessment

Project Overview

The AI Stakeholder Analysis Tool helps policy designers uncover the hidden impacts of proposed policies. Policy failures often happen not because the idea is bad, but because we fail to anticipate how different groups will react to it. This tool uses AI to simulate those reactions before you even publish a draft.

It acts as a "Cost-Benefit Analysis Engine" where custom AI agents adopt the personas of specific interest groups (e.g., small business owners, environmental activists, local residents) to provide critical feedback.

Launch the Stakeholder Analysis Gem

The Intuition: Why Use AI for This?

Standard policy analysis often suffers from blind spots. As a researcher or student, it is difficult to fully step outside your own perspective and imagine the specific grievances or incentives of a stakeholder group you don't belong to.

This tool leverages the "Persona" capabilities of Large Language Models to force that perspective shift. By explicitly instructing an AI to "think like a small business owner concerned about regulations," we can generate arguments and cost-benefit calculations that might otherwise be overlooked.

It turns the solitary act of writing policy into a simulated town hall meeting, allowing you to see the friction points early.

How It Works

The application deploys a multi-step AI workflow:

  1. Gemini Workflow: The Gem will deploy a multi-step workflow inside the conversation.
  2. Stakeholder Identification: It first identifies exactly 3 key groups impacted by your policy.
  3. Simulated Analysis: It performs a Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA) for each group, identifying specific Wins and Losses.
  4. Final Report: It synthesizes these views into a master report with a political feasibility score and suggested amendments.

How to Use It

  1. Launch the Gem: Click the button above to open the Gemini Gem interface (requires a Gemini Advanced subscription).
  2. Enter your proposed policy in the text box.
  3. Review the Consumption: The Gem will generate the analysis as a single, detailed conversation. You can then ask follow-up questions to any of the simulated stakeholders.
