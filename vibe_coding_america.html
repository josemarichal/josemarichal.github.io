Vibe Coding for America

Experimental Course | Public Sector Technology & AI

Course Description

The intersection of artificial intelligence-assisted development, civic domain knowledge, and the practical realities of shipping software in the public sector represents a critical frontier in modern governance. This course addresses a profound systemic tension: civic-minded technologists must learn the labyrinthine constraints of the public domain, while civil servants must be empowered to build and iterate rather than merely procure and monitor.

Resolving this tension requires a paradigm shift from traditional software engineering and top-down policy dictation to "vibe coding"—an outcome-first, AI-assisted development methodology tailored specifically for the rigorous, high-stakes environment of government services.

Schedule

Module 1: The Landscape of Public Sector Technology

Understanding why government IT frequently fails—not due to funding, but due to decoupled policymaking and implementation.

Week 1: Dissecting the Pathology of Failure
  • Jennifer Pahlka, "Recoding America: Why Government Is Failing in the Digital Age"
  • Case Study: California EDD Crisis (COVID-19 Pandemic)
  • Case Study: Healthcare.gov Launch and Rescue
  • Code for America 2021 Impact Report

Module 2: Generative Development and Vibe Coding Fundamentals

Transitioning to the technological solution space: AI-assisted development as an equalizer for domain experts.

Week 2-3: The Vibe Coding Paradigm
  • Tool Comparison: Cursor vs. Replit vs. Bolt.new
  • v0.dev for Generative UI
  • Lovable.dev for Full-Stack React Apps
  • Ink & Switch, "Malleable Software"
  • Claude Artifacts for Rapid Prototyping

Module 3: The Government Data Layer

Grounding generative models in structured, authoritative open government data.

Week 4: API Architecture and Public Data
  • api.data.gov Explorer
  • U.S. Census Bureau API Documentation
  • USAspending.gov API
  • Grants.gov API Resources
  • Department of Commerce: Generative AI and Open Data Best Practices

Module 4: Responsible AI Deployment

Establishing ethical, legal, and operational guardrails for high-stakes contexts.

Week 5: Guardrails and Compliance
  • Section 508 Accessibility Standards
  • U.S. Web Design System (USWDS)
  • Defense Innovation Unit: Responsible AI Guidelines
  • GSA AI Guide for Government

Module 5: Navigating the Institution

Learning to maneuver within procurement laws, budgetary cycles, and organizational behaviors.

Week 6: Procurement Hacks and Bureaucratic Persuasion
  • Micro-purchasing and 18F’s Procurement Hack
  • GSA Cooperative Purchasing Program
  • 18F User Experience Design Guide
  • Trauma-informed User Research and the Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA)

Module 6: Capstone Application

Synthesizing theoretical knowledge into an applied municipal prototype.

Week 7-8: Capstone Project

Projects focused on real-world needs (e.g., Thousand Oaks Zoning Updates or Ventura County Digital Upskilling).

Ecosystem Integration

Learning from institutional champions and the GovTech investment landscape.

  • Profiles: Ron Bronson (Critical Service Design), Angie Quirarte (State Standards)
  • Venture Landscape: Govtech Fund, Thrive Capital, First Round Capital
