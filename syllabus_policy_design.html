Public Policy Design

Special Topic | Fall 2026

Instructor information

Dr. José Marichal (he/him/his)
Professor of Political Science
Contact: marichal@callutheran.edu

Course Description

What happens when we treat public policy not as a set of rules, but as an interaction between the state and its citizens? Inspired by the Copenhagen Institute of Interaction Design (CIID) and the pattern language approach of Jan Borchers, this course applies design thinking, life-centred research, and rapid prototyping to the public policy process.

Students will learn to navigate the complexities of governance by using policy patterns as a lingua franca to bridge the gap between policy makers, technical experts, and citizens. We move beyond traditional policy analysis to focus on the experience of policy—ensuring that services are not just functional, but equitable, human-centric, and systemically aware.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Masters Interaction Design for Policy: Apply IxD fundamental skills and methods to strategic policy interventions.
  • Conduct Life-Centred Research: Use ethnographic and research methods to uncover the lived reality of those affected by policy.
  • Design Across Scales: Understand how policy interventions ripple from individual biological and social scales to global systems.
  • Prototype Policy Interventions: Use physical computing and rapid prototyping to test policy delivery before implementation.
  • Collaborate in Design Teams: Work in multidisciplinary environments to tackle "wicked" public problems.
  • Deploy AI Responsibly: Design policy services that leverage AI and machine learning without compromising ethical standards.
